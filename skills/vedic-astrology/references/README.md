# Vedic Astrology References

## Complete House Significations

### 1st House (Tanu Bhava - Body)
**General**: Self, physical body, personality, temperament, health, general well-being
**Specific**: Head, face, appearance, vitality, ego, self-expression, early childhood
**Karakas**: Sun (atmakaraka - soul), Mars (physical energy)

### 2nd House (Dhana Bhava - Wealth)
**General**: Wealth, family, speech, food, early education, values
**Specific**: Face, right eye, tongue, teeth, accumulated wealth, family traditions
**Karakas**: Jupiter (wealth wisdom), Venus (luxury), Mercury (speech)

### 3rd House (Sahaja Bhava - Siblings)
**General**: Siblings, courage, short journeys, communication, skills
**Specific**: Ears, shoulders, arms, hands, neighbors, hobbies, writing
**Karakas**: Mars (courage), Mercury (communication)

### 4th House (Sukha Bhava - Happiness)
**General**: Mother, home, property, vehicles, inner peace, education
**Specific**: Chest, heart, emotions, domestic comfort, real estate, schooling
**Karakas**: Moon (mother), Venus (comforts), Mercury (education)

### 5th House (Putra Bhava - Children)
**General**: Children, intelligence, creativity, romance, speculation
**Specific**: Stomach, upper abdomen, past life merit, mantras, investments
**Karakas**: Jupiter (children, wisdom), Venus (romance)

### 6th House (Shatru Bhava - Enemies)
**General**: Enemies, diseases, debts, service, obstacles, litigation
**Specific**: Intestines, kidney, servants, pets, competition, daily work
**Karakas**: Mars (enemies), Saturn (chronic disease)

### 7th House (Kalatra Bhava - Spouse)
**General**: Marriage, spouse, partnerships, business, public life
**Specific**: Lower abdomen, sexual organs, trade, agreements, open enemies
**Karakas**: Venus (wife), Jupiter (husband), Mercury (business partner)

### 8th House (Ayu Bhava - Longevity)
**General**: Longevity, inheritance, occult, transformation, sudden events
**Specific**: External sex organs, chronic illness, research, hidden wealth, mysticism
**Karakas**: Saturn (longevity), Mars (sudden events)

### 9th House (Dharma Bhava - Righteousness)
**General**: Father, dharma, fortune, higher education, spirituality, long journeys
**Specific**: Thighs, religious inclinations, gurus, foreign lands, philosophy
**Karakas**: Jupiter (dharma, guru), Sun (father)

### 10th House (Karma Bhava - Profession)
**General**: Career, profession, status, authority, fame, public image
**Specific**: Knees, government position, reputation, livelihood, social standing
**Karakas**: Sun (authority), Mercury (skills), Saturn (labor), Jupiter (advisor)

### 11th House (Labha Bhava - Gains)
**General**: Gains, income, friends, ambitions, elder siblings, fulfillment
**Specific**: Calves, ankles, left ear, profits from career, social network
**Karakas**: Jupiter (expansion), Mercury (commerce)

### 12th House (Vyaya Bhava - Expenses)
**General**: Losses, expenses, foreign lands, spirituality, isolation, liberation
**Specific**: Feet, left eye, bed pleasures, hospitals, ashrams, secret enemies
**Karakas**: Saturn (isolation), Ketu (moksha), Venus (bed pleasures)

## Important Yogas

### Raj Yogas (Royal Combinations)
1. **Dharma-Karma Adhipati Yoga**: Lords of 9th and 10th together
2. **Viparita Raja Yoga**: Dusthana lords (6, 8, 12) in dusthanas
3. **Neecha Bhanga Raja Yoga**: Debilitation cancellation

### Dhana Yogas (Wealth)
1. **Lakshmi Yoga**: 9th lord strong in kendra with Venus
2. **Kubera Yoga**: Lords of 2, 5, 11 together
3. **Wealth from 2-11 Connection**: 2nd & 11th lords in mutual aspect

### Mahapurusha Yogas (Great Person)
1. **Hamsa Yoga**: Jupiter in kendra in own/exaltation
2. **Malavya Yoga**: Venus in kendra in own/exaltation
3. **Sasa Yoga**: Saturn in kendra in own/exaltation
4. **Ruchaka Yoga**: Mars in kendra in own/exaltation
5. **Bhadra Yoga**: Mercury in kendra in own/exaltation

### Difficult Yogas
1. **Kemadruma Yoga**: Moon isolated (no planets in 2nd/12th from Moon)
2. **Kala Sarpa Yoga**: All planets between Rahu-Ketu axis
3. **Grahan Yoga**: Sun-Moon conjunction (eclipse combination)

## KP System Rules

### Cusp Determination
- Most important: Cusp sublord
- Cusp sign and star lord secondary
- House cusps calculated using Placidus/KP system

### Significator Selection (ABCD Method)

**A-Level**: Planets in constellation of house occupant
- Strongest significator
- Direct connection to house

**B-Level**: Planets posited in the house
- Strong significator
- Physical presence in bhava

**C-Level**: Planets in constellation of house lord
- Moderate significator
- Connection through lordship

**D-Level**: House lord itself
- Weakest significator
- Only when no other significators

### Ruling Planets
At moment of judgment:
1. Ascendant sublord
2. Moon sign sublord
3. Moon star sublord
4. Day lord (Vaara)

### Timing Events
Event happens when:
1. Dasha-Bhukti lords are significators
2. Transit supports
3. Ruling planets connect

### House Combinations for Events

**Marriage**: 2, 7, 11 (avoid 1, 6, 10)
**Education**: 4, 9, 11 (avoid 3, 8)
**Career**: 2, 6, 10, 11 (avoid 5, 9)
**Children**: 2, 5, 11 (avoid 1, 4, 10, 11 for female)
**Travel**: 3, 9, 12 (for foreign: 3, 4, 9, 12)
**Health Issues**: 6, 8, 12 (recovery: 1, 11)
**Wealth**: 2, 6, 10, 11 (avoid 5, 8, 12)

## Ashtakavarga Interpretation

### Sarvashtakavarga (SAV)
**Total points in each sign** (all planets combined):
- Above 28 points: Very strong
- 25-28 points: Strong
- 22-24 points: Average
- Below 22 points: Weak

**Transit Results**:
When planet transits high SAV sign: positive results
When planet transits low SAV sign: challenging results

### Bhinnashatkavarga (Individual Planet Points)
**Minimum points for good results**:
- Sun: 4 points
- Moon: 4 points
- Mars: 3 points
- Mercury: 4 points
- Jupiter: 5 points
- Venus: 4 points
- Saturn: 3 points

## Vimshottari Dasha Interpretation

### Mahadasha Results
Based on:
1. Natural significations of planet
2. Houses owned by planet
3. House occupied by planet
4. Aspects received and given
5. Strength (Shadbala, dignity)
6. Conjunctions

### Antardasha Modification
- Friendly antardasha: 75% Maha + 25% Antar
- Neutral antardasha: 50% each
- Inimical antardasha: Can give opposite results

### Critical Periods
- **Sandhi**: Last 1/10th of dasha (transition phase)
- **First year**: Adjustment to new energy
- **Middle period**: Full manifestation
- **Last year**: Winding down

## Classical Text Excerpts

### From Brihat Parashara Hora Shastra

**On Ascendant**:
"The first house indicates the self, life, body, character, conduct, happiness, vitality, and the beginning of life. The Ascendant is the most important factor in the horoscope."

**On 10th House**:
"The 10th house shows one's profession, status in life, honor, father, dignity, governmental favor, self-respect, and karma."

**On Planetary Aspects**:
"All planets aspect the 7th house from themselves. Mars additionally aspects the 4th and 8th. Jupiter aspects the 5th and 9th. Saturn aspects the 3rd and 10th."

### From KP Reader (K.S. Krishnamurti)

**On Significators**:
"A planet in the constellation of the occupant of a house is the strongest significator of that house. Next comes the planet posited in that house. The lord of the constellation where the owner of a house is deposited is the third in order of importance."

**On Timing**:
"An event will fructify when the dasha and bhukti of planets connected with that house or its significators are in operation, provided the sub lord of the cusp promises."

### From Phaladeepika (Mantreswara)

**On Longevity**:
"If the Ascendant, its lord, the Moon, and the 8th house and its lord are all strong and well-placed, the native will live long. If these are weak and afflicted, the life will be short."

**On Wealth**:
"Benefics in the 2nd house or aspecting it, a strong 2nd lord, and benefics in kendras from the 2nd lord give wealth."
